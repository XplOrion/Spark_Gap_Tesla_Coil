# ⚡ Dual MOT Spark Gap Tesla Coil (SGTC)

Simple high-voltage spark-gap Tesla coil built with dual microwave oven transformers (MOTs), ballast bulbs, and basic manual control 
to demonstrate HV generation.

## 🔧 Final Working Setup

* **Input Power**: 220 V AC single-phase ⚡
* **Control**: Manual 250 V AC push-button switch (phase line only) 🔘
* **Ballast**: 3 incandescent bulbs in parallel (current limiting) 💡💡💡
* **Primary**: Dual MOTs connected in parallel 🔌
* **MMC Capacitor**: 36.5 nF total ⚡
* **Secondary Coil**: ~2500–2600 turns (AWG 28 wire) 🧲
* **Primary Coil**: 14 turns 🌀
* **Resonant Frequency**: ~156 kHz (measured) 📡
* **Output**: Full-length visible streamer arcs when push-button is pressed ⚡⚡

## 🪵 Enclosure

The entire Tesla coil assembly is mounted inside a **hexagonal wooden enclosure** for structural support and safer demonstration during operation. The enclosure organizes the MOTs, ballast bulbs, primary circuit, and secondary coil in a compact layout.

## 💡 Wireless Power Demonstration

Two LEDs are used to demonstrate **wireless energy transmission** from the Tesla coil's electric field.

When the coil is operating, the LEDs light up without any direct electrical connection, illustrating the principle of **wireless power transfer**.

## ⚡ Arc / Earth Electrode

A **PVC pipe with a metal nail inserted at the tip** is used as a grounded electrode.
The nail is connected to **earth ground** and positioned close to the **topload sphere** of the Tesla coil.

When the coil operates, high-voltage streamers preferentially strike this grounded nail, producing **controlled visible arcs**.

## 🔌 Wiring Overview

* Mains Phase (Live) → push-button switch → ballast bulbs → dual MOT primaries
* Mains Neutral → dual MOT primaries (direct connection)

## ⚡ Result

Press the push-button → coil powers up → long streamer arcs appear.
Release → coil turns off.

Simple, reliable and full arc performance achieved.
